Mental Model

What IS a Gaussian Splat?

Imagine a cloud of millions of fuzzy colored blobs floating in 3D space. Each blob has position, size, shape, color, and transparency. When rendered from any angle, these blobs align to recreate what the cameras saw.

Traditional 3D
Polygons → Hard edges
NeRF
Neural Network → Slow
Gaussian Splats
Point Cloud → Fast ✓

The breakthrough: Gaussians are differentiable—you can use gradient descent to optimize millions of blob parameters until they match your camera images.

Demystified

The Training Loop

That's the whole algorithm. Everything else is optimization tricks.

0 START: Random fuzzy blobs scattered in space
1 Pick a camera viewpoint
2 Render what the blobs would look like from there
3 Compare to the ACTUAL photo from that camera
4 Calculate error (difference between render and photo)
5 Adjust blob positions/colors/sizes to reduce error
6 Repeat 7,000-30,000 times
✓ END: Blobs now arranged to perfectly recreate the scene
Where We Can Win

Unsolved Problems

These are real gaps in the tech that a scrappy team could crack.

01

The Temporal Problem

Current methods train each frame independently (slow, huge files) or use complex 4D representations (research-grade). What if we stored keyframe splats + motion deltas? Like video compression for 3D.

02

The Streaming Problem

Nobody has cracked efficient streaming of 4D splats. Solutions load everything upfront. Opportunity: progressive LOD loading, predictive prefetching based on user behavior.

03

Camera Efficiency

8 cameras is expensive. Research uses 50-100+. Can we train quality splats from fewer angles using learned priors, depth sensors, or temporal information?

04

Real-Time Training

What if capture and training happened simultaneously? Camera feed → Edge GPU → Live splat stream → Viewers. Revolutionary for live content.
